1.0 Executive Summary

This document provides a technical overview of the Hydro-Combustion Supercritical Purifier (HCSP), a modular, closed-loop water treatment system. It is specifically designed to treat wastewater streams from indoor agriculture, such as nutrient-rich runoff and intermittent cleaning-in-place (CIP) waste. The system utilizes a process of electrolysis and clean hydrogen combustion to power Supercritical Water Oxidation (SCWO). This technology transforms complex wastewater into two manageable outputs: distilled-quality water suitable for reuse or compliant discharge, and a small volume of contained, inert solid sludge. The process requires no chemical additives and produces no stack pollutants like NOx, as the primary emission is water vapor. This technology offers a robust and reliable method for indoor farms to close their water loop, significantly reducing freshwater intake and ensuring a clean discharge.

2.0 Process Description

The HCSP operates on a continuous, four-stage cycle that is fully automated and controlled. The diagram below illustrates the core process flow.

1. Electrolysis

Pure water is split into H₂ and O₂ gas using electricity.

2. Combustion

H₂ and O₂ are ignited, producing intense, clean heat.

3. SCWO

Heat creates supercritical water, destroying contaminants.

4. Separation

Pure water is condensed; solids are collected as sludge.

3.0 System Specifications (10 m³/day Module)

The following specifications are typical for a standard HCSP skid-mounted module designed for an indoor agricultural facility.

Parameter Value (Typical) Notes
Throughput Capacity 10 m³/day (~10,000 L/day) Operates continuously or in scheduled batches.
Core Technology Supercritical Water Oxidation (SCWO) Powered by internal hydrogen combustion.
Water Recovery 85–95% to distillate Balance becomes sludge, dependent on feed concentration.
Output Water Quality Distilled water; <10 µS/cm conductivity Can be remineralized for reuse or discharged.
Operating Conditions 380–420 °C; ~25 MPa (250 bar) Well within the supercritical regime.
Average Electrical Load ~75–95 kW Includes electrolyzer and auxiliary equipment.
Daily H₂ Consumption ~30–40 kg/day Generated and consumed on-demand from water.
Physical Footprint ~8–10 m² floor area; ≤3 m height Designed to fit in typical mechanical rooms.
Noise Level <75 dBA at 1 meter Contained within enclosed skids.

4.0 Regulatory, Environmental, and Safety Profile

The HCSP is engineered for safe, unattended operation and to meet stringent regulatory standards.

  • Water Discharge: The primary output is distilled water, which can be verified online with conductivity and TOC proxy sensors. This water is suitable for reuse within the facility (e.g., fertigation make-up) or for compliant discharge.
  • Waste Handling: All separated contaminants are contained as a stable, inert sludge in a collection chamber. This sludge is periodically removed via standard waste services in accordance with all local regulations.
  • Air Emissions: The system requires no air permit for combustion byproducts. The sealed combustion of hydrogen and pure oxygen produces only water vapor, with negligible NOx formation. Excess oxygen is safely vented.
  • Safety & Controls: The system is fully automated with a PLC/industrial controller and features redundant safety interlocks. This includes multi-sensor monitoring for temperature, pressure, and hydrogen detection, as well as pressure relief valves and an automatic safe shutdown protocol. The closed-loop hydrogen system produces fuel on demand, eliminating risks associated with bulk storage of combustible gases.
