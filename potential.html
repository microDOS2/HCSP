Executive Overview

The Hydro-Combustion Supercritical Purifier (HCSP) addresses the economics of produced water treatment by providing 97–99% water recovery at scale. At the $5/bbl benchmark, a 1M bbl/day unit displaces up to $1.825B annually in disposal costs. Unlike trucking and SWD, HCSP creates distilled-quality water ready for reuse, with minimal sludge and optional recovery of salts/metals. These recovered salts and metals can themselves be marketed, creating secondary income streams that enhance project economics and provide investors with downstream value opportunities.

ROI and IRR

HCSP’s ROI profile is exceptional: payback occurs in under 12 months even with conservative assumptions. At 90% utilization, annual net savings exceed $1.31B. IRR calculations exceed 500% at 5 years and pass 1000% at 10 years, reflecting extraordinary capital efficiency. Importantly, ROI further benefits from potential revenues tied to the marketing of recovered salts and metals, which diversify and expand income streams beyond water treatment alone.

Purchase Model

Ownership requires upfront CAPEX (~$12.5M: $10M build, $1.5M delivery, $1M install). Annual fixed OPEX totals $0.75M. Variable OPEX runs ~$1.50/bbl. Customers retain all avoided disposal and freshwater offset value, resulting in ~$1.31B annual net savings at 90% utilization. In addition, marketing of recovered salts and metals can yield further revenues, turning HCSP into both a cost-avoidance and a revenue-generating asset.

Service / Lease

Service agreements avoid CAPEX, instead using ~$5.5M annual fees. Customers still net ~$1.308B annually after fees and OPEX. This model suits operators seeking capital-light strategies. Providers capture steady service revenue with IRR depending on utilization and contract term. Customers may also benefit from shared revenues if salts and metals recovery is integrated, aligning provider and customer incentives.

Secondary Revenues from Salts & Metals Recovery

Beyond cost avoidance, HCSP enables capture of salts and metals present in produced water. These materials can be marketed in industrial and specialty applications. Depending on feed composition and recovery efficiency, potential revenues may range from $0.10–$0.50 per barrel treated. At 328.5M bbl/year throughput, this equates to an additional $32.8M–$164.3M annually, enhancing IRR and shortening payback.
